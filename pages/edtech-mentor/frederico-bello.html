Breaking the Textbook Monopoly with Luca: Frederico Bello's Mission to Redefine K-12 Education for Latin America

FoundersSeries

Frederico Bello, Founder & CEO at Luca

In a region with over 120 million K-12 students, Latin America stands as the third-largest educational market globally, trailing only India and China. It's a landscape ripe for disruption, and Frederico Bello, founder of Luca, is leading the charge. In this EdTech Mentor conversation, hosted by Laureano Díaz, CSO of 27zero, Frederico shares how Luca is reshaping education with AI-driven innovation, hybrid solutions, and a bold mission to dethrone legacy publishers.

On the journey into EdTech

Hi Frederico, thank you for joining the EdTech Mentor series. Can you tell us about your journey into EdTech and the company you currently lead?

I'm the founder of Luca, originally from Portugal. My career started in investment banking at Morgan Stanley, working in M&A in London — little to do with EdTech, though I covered some education companies. Over time I realized I didn't want to climb the traditional corporate ladder in finance. I worked at Uber in Europe launching new cities, and co-founded an electric scooter business in Portugal and Spain. When that company was acquired, I saw an opportunity to explore a new sector.

Two things led me to found an education company: I enjoyed working on early-stage projects, and I wanted my work to have tangible impact. I later led the international expansion of a Spanish EdTech company, Power MBA, which offered a $1,500 online MBA as an alternative to traditional $50,000 programs. That success, during COVID, triggered my decision to start Luca about three years ago.

Changing the Game of Educational Publishing

Luca aims to be the leading educational publisher in Latin America within the next 10 years — a $200 billion market ripe for disruption, integrating digital and physical content to reshape learning experiences.

On choosing K-12 as the entry point

Why did you pick K-12 as your initial market?

While leading Power MBA's international expansion across more than 20 countries, I noticed how mature the EdTech market was in India, with a strong bias toward K-12 — companies serving that sector were growing rapidly and raising venture funding. I strongly believed K-12 would remain the most dynamic sector during and after the pandemic, and that belief became the genesis of Luca.

What Luca does today

What does Luca do, and what's your vision for the company?

Luca is a B2B SaaS platform for schools. We provide academic content and classroom management tools — teachers access a library covering the full official curriculum for core subjects, with tools to track homework, evaluate performance, and identify learning gaps. You can think of Luca as the "textbook publisher of the future." All our content is created with AI, which also helps teachers save time on administrative tasks. We've expanded into physical materials too — books and manipulative tools for math learning based on the Singapore methodology.

Faster Adoption Than Ever

Within the first week of implementation, 90% of teachers actively use Luca with at least five daily actions. Luca delivers academic improvements of 25–40% over traditional methodologies, backed by a study led by TEC de Monterrey.

On the Latin American K-12 market

Can you give us an overview of the private school market in Mexico?

Latin America is the third-largest K-12 market globally, with around 120 million students — behind India's 300 million and China's 200 million. Mexico alone has about 30 million students, 20% in private schools, with roughly 20,000 private schools capable of adopting technology. The majority are mid-to-lower-middle-tier schools, with monthly tuition typically ranging from 1,000 to 4,000 Mexican pesos.

The biggest challenges these schools face: cost (legacy publishers charge roughly double what Luca does), stakeholder perception (parents often doubt whether existing materials are used effectively), and legacy publishers' inability to demonstrate measurable academic improvement.

The Key to Growth: Brand Awareness

If I had to pick one marketing focus, it would be brand awareness — building trust through events, advertising, and success stories strengthens our outbound strategy.

On AI and content production

Can you tell us more about how Luca leverages artificial intelligence?

Two main areas. First, generative AI for content production — most images in our textbooks are created using tools like DALL·E and refined by our team, enabling rapid, cost-effective standardization. Second, reactive digital experiences that solve common teacher challenges: automated grading, correction of written assignments, and generation of interdisciplinary projects. We recently introduced a conversational bot for English learning, where students practice with an AI playing a 17th-century history professor — removing the social anxiety of speaking in front of peers.

On marketing and growth

If you had to choose just one marketing workstream for Luca right now, which would it be?

Brand awareness. We operate as a B2B company, so it's key to support our outbound channel — a company calls, sparks interest, but what's next? Then you see an email campaign, notice ads, attend an event, or hear a case study about another school's success. Positioning Luca as an expert in Education 2.0 or 3.0 through awareness initiatives would be my top priority.

Has outbound been the channel with the highest return on marketing investment?

Not the highest return on marketing investment, but definitely the highest return on staff investment — for every euro spent on salaries, we generate six euros in sales within a year. Only now, after building brand recognition and gaining tens of thousands of users, have we shifted toward marketing: more events, talks, and SEO investment.

Shattering Skepticism

The main objection is that schools haven't heard of us before — distrust ties directly back to why brand awareness is so critical. Luca works with just a simple projector; no expensive tech investment required.

Looking ahead

What's your ultimate vision for Luca?

To become the leading educational publisher in Latin America within the next 10 years, with significant expansion plans for the United States and Europe. Education in Latin America mirrors patterns seen in fintech, healthcare, and transportation, where long-established players with little incentive to innovate dominate the market. Our high NPS and low churn — 8% last year — combined with a sales cycle under 60 days, create a solid foundation for growth.

For marketing, the challenge is positioning yourself as an underdog with a superior product but lower market share. I believe the education market is nearing the point where bold, creative marketing becomes a necessity — an exciting opportunity for collaboration between agencies and EdTech companies.
